     Social media platforms are the best way to connect around the globe, with just a few strokes on the keyboard and the click of the mouse you can talk to someone thousands of miles away. There are many pros and cons to this type of technology. Steven Luckert stated in his article Extremists Are Thriving on Social Media. How Should We Respond? “In recent years, ISIS has enlisted thousands of foreign fighters through its online activities, notably via slick videos posted on YouTube and other Internet channels” (Huffington Post January 25, 2017) ISIS recruiters have been using the vast amount of personal information available online to target and recruit individuals who are like-minded or easily targeted and manipulated.
     There are also many less extreme instances where average people are polarized or driven into more extreme political views because they are surrounded by polarizing information. Social Media algorithms are designed to give users content that they are interested in or enjoy consuming. A side effect of this is users are only shown information that they agree with, preventing them from seeing other points of view, which can lead to users becoming more polarized. Learn More
